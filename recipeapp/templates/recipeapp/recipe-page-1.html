Jollof Fried Rice

  • Serves: 6 people
  • Prep Time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking: 1 hours
  • Calories: 284 cal

Jollof rice is a rich and incredibly tasty west African one-pot Meal. It is a very versatile dish, and it is usually made from scratch using Rice, Tomatoes, Pimento peppers, Tomato paste, Scotch bonnet, Onions, Salt, and other spices.

Ingredients

Directions

  1. Season the beef with salt, garlic powder and ¼ tsp oil. Let it marinate for about 10 minutes.
  2. Heat a wok with 1/2 tbsp oil. Add the beef and brown. Once brown, remove from the work and set aside. Season the shrimp with black pepper and salt. Add the butter into the same wok used for the beef. Once the butter is melted, add the shrimp and stir fry until pink. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add 1tbsp of oil to the wok, chop in the onions and add the thyme and cook until the onion translucent. Add the veggies and stir fry for about 3 minutes.
  4. Add the ginger and the atadindin sauce(if the sauce is too thick, loosen with 1tbsp water or stock). Stir fry for about 1-2 minutes; then add the rice, shrimp and beef. Stir fry for about 3-5 minutes.
  5. Check for seasonings add the basil or scent leaves and enjoy...

This is one of my favorite recipes this year! It’s like a redo of my Jollof fried rice recipe; only with the use of Atadindin. The beauty of this recipe is that, you can make it with any spicy Nigerian stew. The beauty also lies in the combination of the spicy stew with basil. Ordinarily, if you have had Efirin/basil stew, you will know the deliciousness that it carries with it.
